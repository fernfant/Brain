# WBR Week 10 (Mar — Conflict Impact)

## Summary
The first WBR dominated by the US-Iran conflict. Both Primary and Secondary fell in value and volume, with Secondary declining more steeply. March sales forecast collapsed from 95% to 66% in a single week. New Projects took the sharpest hit — sessions –36.5%, sold leads –45.8%, revenue –42% WoW. The only bright spot: SEO SoV jumped +3.9pp to 45.3%, with particular strength in Rent and Generic keywords. App and web metrics were unavailable due to simultaneous Kochava and CHEQ implementation failures.

## Key Points
- US-Iran conflict triggered steep declines in real estate demand and transactions
- March sales FC: collapsed from 95% to 66% (upside limited to 69%)
- New Projects: sessions –36.5%, sold leads –45.8%, revenue –42% WoW
- SEO SoV: 45.3% (+3.9pp WoW) — strong gain despite broader shock
- App/web metrics unavailable: Kochava and CHEQ issues coincided
- Primary Plus campaigns: only –3% since conflict start — resilient segment
- Media & Developers: new budgets placed on hold

## Key Insights

- **The forecast collapse from 95% to 66% in one week quantifies how binary the shock was**: A 29-percentage-point forecast cut in seven days is not a gradual deterioration — it's a cliff. Businesses with gradual exposure can hedge and adjust; this event offered no lead time. The fact that the March forecast had been at 95% heading into the conflict week means the pipeline looked nearly clean, making the drop even more disorienting for sales teams with active client conversations.

- **SEO SoV gaining +3.9pp during a demand crash is a structural strength signal**: When paid media pulls back and consumer intent searches drop, organic search typically holds or even gains share — users who are still searching are more likely to use search engines directly. The Rent (+7.1pp) and Generic (+2.35pp) gains in W10 suggest that even in a shock week, PF's organic presence held and strengthened. This validates the importance of sustained SEO investment as a counter-cyclical asset.

- **Primary Plus declining only 3% while New Projects dropped 42% reveals a segmentation insight**: Primary Plus is a B2B product sold to developers for premium placement — a recurring commitment. New Projects revenue is more transactional and sentiment-sensitive. The –3% vs –42% gap means developers with locked-in contracts kept spending even as consumer demand evaporated. This suggests revenue predictability varies dramatically by product type within the same market shock.

- **Simultaneous Kochava and CHEQ failures during the most critical measurement week of the quarter is a significant governance failure**: The one week you most need accurate data to understand the impact of a crisis — week one of a major geopolitical shock — is exactly the week that two separate data systems failed at once. This is either an extraordinary coincidence (CHEQ was mid-implementation) or a sign that infrastructure changes were being rushed without adequate stability testing.
