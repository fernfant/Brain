# WBR Week 4 (Jan Month-End)

## Summary
January closed near perfectly commercially — AED 63.2M, 99.5% of forecast — against a market backdrop running +58% YoY. New Projects outperformed across every metric. But the supply story soured: Exclusive Listings stagnated again at 16K, and crucially, Bayut signed new-to-market inventory during the week, directly widening the competitive supply gap. SEO SoV dipped due to SEMrush tracking issues compounding ranking volatility in rental and community keywords.

## Key Points
- DLD market: +58% YoY (Primary +65%, Secondary +52%) — extraordinary month
- January sales closed: AED 63.2M (99.5% of forecast)
- New Projects: $175.9K revenue; MtD +155% YoY
- Net Exclusive Listings: 16K — stagnated after W3 jump; Bayut signed new inventory this week
- SEO SoV dipped — SEMrush tracking issues + rental/community keyword volatility
- PR SoV: 71% (well above 50–55% target)
- App stability: Apdex >95% on both Android and iOS
- Feb/March forecast: 90–95%

## Key Insights

- **January's market growth masks a normalization risk**: DLD Sale Value at +58% YoY (Primary +65%) is extraordinary, but much of this is driven by land and >AED 10M transactions — strip those out and growth was +30% YoY. A business forecasting into February at 90–95% was already assuming deceleration. The gap between headline market numbers and normalised growth is a recurring pattern in UAE real estate that distorts planning if not adjusted for.

- **Bayut signing new-to-market inventory in the same week listings stagnated is the competitive risk crystallising**: While PF's 16K Exclusive Listings stagnated for the second time, Bayut wasn't standing still — it was actively acquiring supply. Every week Bayut signs new exclusives is a week the 29% listing gap widens. This is not a lagging indicator; it's a live competitive action happening while PF's supply pipeline is static.

- **SEO SoV dipping at month-end raises measurement reliability questions**: The combination of SEMrush tracking issues and ranking volatility means January's SEO exit rate is unreliable. Starting February with a questionable SEO baseline makes it hard to evaluate whether the subsequent weeks' gains (42.2% in W6, 42.8% in W3) represent real improvement or recovery from a measurement trough.
