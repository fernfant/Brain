# WBR Week 11 (Mar — Conflict Continues)

## Summary
Week two of conflict conditions. Transaction values improved slightly WoW but leading indicators (Form F, Rentals) showed no sentiment recovery, suggesting the market had stabilised at a lower level rather than bouncing back. March forecast held at 65% (upside 68%). New Projects re-entered the green at 102% of weekly target — but the recovery was driven by price increases, not volume, an important distinction. Operationally, the week delivered genuine positives: App DAU recovered +17% WoW, Mobile INP hit its 72% target for the first time, and B2B incidents reached a record low (17 MtD).

## Key Points
- Market: slightly improved WoW but leading indicators show no sentiment recovery
- March FC: 65% (upside 68%); cashflow concerns top of client minds
- New Projects: 102% of weekly target — recovery via higher pricing, not volume
- SEO SoV: 45.8% (+0.5pp WoW)
- App DAU: 50.9K (+17% WoW) — recovering from prior week dip
- Mobile INP: 72.0% — hit target for the first time this quarter
- B2B: Record-low incident volume (17 MtD)
- Kochava vs Redshift and GA4 vs Redshift data discrepancies flagged

## Key Insights

- **New Projects recovering via price, not volume, is a fragile recovery**: Revenue at 102% of target despite lower sold lead volumes means average transaction value increased — likely because only the most committed buyers transacted, and developers priced for the smaller pool. This is a pattern that works for one or two weeks but is unsustainable at scale: if volume doesn't recover, pricing power will eventually yield to demand pressure. The W12 recovery to $168.9K confirms the trend held short-term, but it's worth watching whether volume catches up.

- **Mobile INP hitting 72% for the first time after a quarter of misses is a meaningful milestone, even mid-crisis**: Engineering delivered on a performance target during one of the most operationally disrupted weeks of the quarter. This signals the INP improvement programme wasn't deprioritised when the business was under pressure — a positive sign for product discipline.

- **Leading indicators showing no recovery while transactional values improved is a warning about false bottoms**: Form F (off-plan reservation forms) and rental inquiries are demand leading indicators — they predict near-term transaction activity. If these were still at conflict-level lows while transaction values ticked up, the W11 improvement may reflect pent-up pre-conflict deal closures rather than genuine demand recovery. The subsequent W12 forecast remaining at 66% suggests this concern was warranted.
