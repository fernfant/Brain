# WBR Week 6 (Feb ~8)

## Summary
First full week of February. The market was growing +15% YoY and commercial tracking was strong at 97% of the AED 62.6M target. Two significant actions were announced: a rebaselining of Exclusive Listings to remove non-compliant and duplicate entries (7 action items across Feb–Apr), and the plan to implement CHEQ anti-spam by Feb 24. A marketing feed disruption on Feb 7–8 caused a sharp but brief traffic crash (–54% web sessions, –40% leads), recovering by Feb 9.

## Key Points
- DLD Sale market: +15% YoY MtD (Primary +17%, Secondary +13%)
- Sales forecast: 97% of AED 62.6M target
- Exclusive Listings rebaselining announced: non-compliant/duplicate listings to be removed; 7 action items
- SEO SoV: 42.2% (+1.1pp WoW)
- Feb 7–8 marketing feed disruption: –54% web sessions, –40% leads; recovered Feb 9
- App installs: 22.9K (+50% vs target, +45.5% YoY)
- Mobile INP Good Rate: 70.6% vs 72% target
- B2B: App active agents 10,440; incident volumes in green

## Key Insights

- **Exclusive Listings rebaselining is an admission, not just a cleanup**: Announcing a 7-item programme to remove non-compliant and duplicate listings implicitly acknowledges that the 16K figure being reported was inflated. Once the cleanup completes, the reported count will likely drop before it can grow — meaning the competitive listings gap to Bayut will appear to widen on paper, even if data quality improves. How leadership communicates this transition is as important as the operational fix.

- **The Feb 7–8 marketing feed disruption illustrates single-point-of-failure risk**: A one-day disruption caused a –54% session crash and –40% lead decline on web paid. Recovery was fast (by Feb 9), but the event reveals how concentrated web paid traffic is on a single feed mechanism. Any outage in a high-competition week (like a new project launch) could directly cost revenue.

- **App installs at +50% above target signals growing consumer pull**: With installs at 22.9K (+45.5% YoY), the consumer funnel was genuinely accelerating in early February — not just recovering from a trough. This is the environment that made February's eventual App MAU of 454K possible. The contrast with Mobile INP still below 72% suggests users were coming despite product performance issues, not because of them.
