# WBR Week 13 (Apr — Eid Relief)

## Summary
First week of April. The business entered a new phase: geopolitical hostilities ongoing but the commercial response is now in full swing — March Relief packages active, Eid Relief packages announced, 79% of the client book in active conversations. New Projects surged to $168.9K (+29% above weekly target, +45.4% WoW), the strongest single-week result since pre-conflict. App metrics showed continued recovery. The dominant concern shifted from demand shock management to app store ranking stability, with both iOS and Android experiencing significant position drops due to Google Play policy changes.

## Key Points
- April FC: 53% (upside 55%); ongoing hostilities driving churn and limiting upside
- 79% of client book in active conversations on relief packages
- New Projects: $168.9K (+29% above weekly target, +45.4% WoW)
- Overall SoS: 53.4%; App SoS: 58.0% (above target); Web SoS: 46.1% (recovering)
- App DAU: 54.4K (+5% WoW); WAU: 204.4K
- iOS App Store: PF dropped from #4 to #7 in Lifestyle; Bayut at #4
- Android: PF dropped from #2 to #51; Bayut from #1 to #47 — Google Play policy impact
- 3 Q2 actions planned to address app rating sustainability
- Anti-crisis broker playbook shared with Business Consultants

## Key Insights

- **New Projects' +45.4% WoW recovery from a –42% crash in W10 shows it was demand suppression, not structural damage**: The speed of the rebound — from 56% below WoW to 45% above in three weeks — indicates that developer off-plan demand was deferred by the conflict, not destroyed. Buyers who paused resumed quickly once perceived stability returned. This is very different from secondary market dynamics and represents a genuinely more resilient revenue stream.

- **The April 53% forecast with 79% of clients in relief conversations means the recovery is being subsidised**: Nearly four-fifths of the client book is now receiving pricing relief to prevent churn. This preserves relationships but compresses margin. When relief packages expire, the business faces a re-pricing moment — clients who received discounts will benchmark future pricing against the relief rate, not the pre-conflict rate. The revenue recovery visible in April metrics is partly funded by future pricing power.

- **App store ranking drops on both platforms in the same week is a platform risk event, not a competitive one**: Both PF and Bayut dropped dramatically on Android (PF: #2 to #51; Bayut: #1 to #47), confirming the cause is Google Play policy rather than PF-specific action. But PF slipping from #4 to #7 on iOS while Bayut holds #4 is a relative competitive disadvantage — and it's happening at exactly the moment PF needs maximum consumer acquisition to rebuild demand post-conflict. The three Q2 rating actions need to be tracked with urgency.

- **App DAU recovering to 54.4K with WAU at 204.4K while April forecast is only 53% reveals a consumer-commercial disconnect**: Consumer engagement (app usage, sessions, DAU) is recovering faster than commercial outcomes (revenue, forecast %). Users are returning to browse, but the conversion from browsing to transaction is being suppressed by market uncertainty. This gap — engaged consumers not converting — is the defining dynamic of the recovery phase and is likely to close gradually as confidence returns.
