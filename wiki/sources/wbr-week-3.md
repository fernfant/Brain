# WBR Week 3 (Jan)

## Summary
A standout week commercially: DLD market at +28% YoY, sales hit 100% of the AED 63.5M target, and New Projects delivered extraordinary results (+132.9% sold leads YoY MtD, +169.7% revenue YoY MtD). Exclusive Listings jumped to 16.1K after NB Seal offers were approved, ending the three-week stagnation. The main concern was data reliability — Kochava tracking failures on Jan 15 and 18 corrupted app session data and downstream LPL calculations.

## Key Points
- DLD market: +28% YoY (Primary +34%, Secondary +21%)
- Sales: 100% forecast achievement (AED 63.5M)
- Net Exclusive Listings: 16.1K (+4.4K WoW) — driven by NB Seal offer approvals
- SEO SoV: 42.8% (+1.2pp WoW)
- New Projects: sold leads +132.9% YoY MtD; revenue +169.7% YoY MtD
- Kochava outage on Jan 15 & 18 — app sessions and LPL calculations impacted
- 29% listing gap vs competition remains despite supply jump

## Key Insights

- **The listings jump was event-driven, not structural**: The 4.4K WoW increase in Exclusive Listings came entirely from NB Seal offer approvals — not from improved agent compliance or organic supply growth. Without a follow-on approval event, stagnation was predictable, and the 29% listing gap to competition persisted regardless. Supply capture needs a systematic solution, not approval bursts.

- **New Projects' YoY growth rates (+169.7% revenue) need context**: A MtD revenue figure of +169.7% YoY sounds extraordinary but likely reflects both genuine demand growth and a low base from January 2024. Even so, the magnitude indicates that off-plan developer product was the most dynamic growth engine early in the year — and the eventual W10 shock to this segment (–42% WoW) would prove painful precisely because expectations had been set so high.

- **Kochava is a single point of failure for key metrics**: Two tracking failures in one week (Jan 15 & 18) corrupted both app sessions and LPL calculations. LPL (leads per listing) is used in commercial client discussions — data gaps here aren't just a measurement problem, they're a revenue conversation problem. The recurrence of Kochava issues across the quarter (W3, W10, W11) suggests no durable fix was implemented after the first failure.
