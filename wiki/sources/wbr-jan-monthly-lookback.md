# WBR January Monthly Lookback

## Summary
January's monthly review confirmed a strong commercial close (AED 63.2M, 99.5% of target) in a market running +63% YoY — though normalised growth excl. land and >AED 10M transactions was a more moderate +30% YoY. The App SoS spike to 64.4% (+11pp MoM) was flagged for validation. Two structural concerns emerged: Net Exclusive Listings at 16K vs a 23K target (a 7K gap), and B2C NPS at 3 — down 9 points MoM and 10 points YoY.

## Key Points
- DLD Sale: +63% YoY (Primary +90%, Secondary +38%); excl. land/>AED 10M: +30%
- Sales: AED 63.2M (99.5% vs target); Feb FC 95.7%, March FC 92.7%
- Net Exclusive Listings: 16K vs 23K target — 7K shortfall
- Overall SoS: 57.8% (+6.6pp MoM); App SoS 64.4% (+11pp — under validation)
- SEO SoS: 45.8% — below target, not recovered from November decline
- New Projects Primary Plus: 125% of target
- App MAU: 430K (+9% YoY); Installs: 106K (+18% YoY)
- B2C NPS: 3 (–9 MoM, –10 YoY)
- CHEQ anti-spam implementation planned by Feb 24

## Key Insights

- **The 7K Exclusive Listings gap is a structural miss, not a timing miss**: Ending January at 16K vs a 23K target — a 30% shortfall — means the target was never realistic given the pipeline, or supply capture was fundamentally underperforming. Given Bayut was actively signing new-to-market inventory (noted in W4), the gap likely reflects a competitive dynamic that point-in-time initiatives (NB Seal approvals, rebaselining) can't fully close without a sustained supply strategy.

- **B2C NPS at 3, down 10 YoY, is a pre-conflict product health warning**: This NPS reading predates the geopolitical shock by two months. A score of 3 — effectively neutral-to-negative — occurring during a bull market (DLD +63% YoY) suggests the user experience deteriorated independent of external conditions. Strong usage metrics (MAU +9%, installs +18%) and a poor NPS together indicate people are using PF because they have to, not because they love it.

- **The App SoS spike to 64.4% should not anchor future targets**: The +11pp MoM jump was flagged under validation, likely reflecting CHEQ anti-spam work not yet complete and possible attribution noise from Kochava. Using 64.4% as a baseline for February App SoS targets would set a misleading high bar. The eventual February App SoS of 56.4% and March range of 50–57% suggest 55–58% is the realistic operating range.

- **SEO not recovering from November is a quiet risk**: SEO SoS at 45.8%, noted as not recovered since November, means the business entered a strong commercial January with a weakening organic channel. Given that the SEO snapshot report (March 2026) shows PF at 61% organic traffic share, the gap between the WBR SoS figures and the SEO report's share figure likely reflects different measurement methodologies — but the non-recovery trend is worth monitoring.
