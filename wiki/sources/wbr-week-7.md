# WBR Week 7 (Feb)

## Summary
Mid-February: commercial forecast holding at 90% (AED 56.5M vs 62.6M target), with New Projects back in green (+18.5% vs target). The week was marked by product issues: Android app rating dropped from 4.3 to 4.2 as Google removed legitimate 5-star reviews, an iOS Apdex dip due to a Luciq SDK bug, and Mobile INP still below target — raising the question of whether strong install growth was being offset by degrading app quality perception.

## Key Points
- Sales forecast: 90% (AED 56.5M vs 62.6M target)
- New Projects: $171.4K (+18.5% vs target); Feb forecast >100%
- SEO SoV: 41.6% — Buy 65.3% (–4.3pp WoW), Rent 47.8% (–1.7pp), Generic 29.0% (+1.4pp)
- App installs: 24.3K (+61% vs target); MAU: 456K
- Android rating: 4.2 — Google removed legitimate 5-star ratings
- iOS Apdex: 91.7% vs 95% target — Luciq SDK issue, recovery in progress
- Mobile INP Good Rate: 69.6% vs 72% target
- B2B: App active agents 10,676; incident volumes within target range

## Key Insights

- **The Google rating removal is a platform governance risk, not a product quality event**: Losing legitimate 5-star reviews to Google's spam algorithm creates a false signal in the market — the rating dropped from 4.3 to 4.2 through no fault of the product. But to potential users, a falling rating looks the same regardless of cause. The risk is that it happens repeatedly (as it did — three separate incidents across the quarter) and PF's app store presence trends downward in a period when app installs were otherwise accelerating.

- **SEO Buy segment dropping –4.3pp in a single week demands explanation**: The Buy segment falling from ~69.6% to 65.3% WoW while Generic gained +1.4pp suggests possible keyword ranking shifts in the highest-value segment (Sale drives 85% of commission pool). A one-week swing of this magnitude in Buy SoV is material and warrants investigation into whether Bayut made ranking gains or whether PF experienced a technical/content issue.

- **Strong install growth (+61% vs target) during sustained Mobile INP underperformance is a temporary state**: Users are arriving in large numbers, but the app is still failing to hit its performance target (72% INP Good Rate). This combination cannot hold indefinitely — either performance improves, or install growth will slow as word-of-mouth and ratings reflect the experience. The Luciq SDK and INP issues together suggest Q1 product quality debt was accumulating during a strong commercial period.
