# WBR Week 12 (Mar)

## Summary
Third week of conflict conditions. The overall market remained ~24% below prior year with no recovery in sentiment leading indicators. March forecast held at 66%. The week's defining challenge was Exposure LPL (eLPL): Buy eLPL at 3.7 vs a 7.6 target — a 51% lead shortfall — with Rent similarly distressed at 7.1 vs 11. Q2 eLPL targets will be reset. App SoS recovered strongly to 57.1% (+6.1pp WoW), but Web SoS fell to 45.2% (–3.1pp WoW, –4.8pp vs target), widening the app-web divergence.

## Key Points
- Market: ~24% below prior year; no sentiment recovery in leading indicators
- March FC: 66%; cashflow concerns persist
- App SoS: 57.1% (+6.1pp WoW, above target); Web SoS: 45.2% (–4.8pp vs target)
- SEO SoV: 45.8% flat; Buy 63.8% (+1.1pp), Generic 35.4% (+2.5pp WoW)
- Exposure LPL: Buy eLPL 3.7 vs 7.6 target (–51% leads); Rent 7.1 vs 11 (–43% leads)
- Q2 eLPL targets to be reset
- New Projects: 89% of weekly target; MtD +34.9% YoY
- Android rating: recovering after Google removed spam ratings

## Key Insights

- **Exposure LPL at half of target is the most client-visible impact of the conflict**: eLPL is the metric clients see when they log into the platform — it tells them how many leads their listings are generating. Buy eLPL at 3.7 vs a 7.6 target (–51%) means clients are seeing lead volumes that are roughly half what they were promised. This directly drives churn and feeds the demand for relief packages. The decision to reset Q2 targets is the right call, but it also sets a low bar that will make future improvement harder to celebrate.

- **Web SoS declining while App SoS recovers is not a neutral split**: App SoS recovering to 57.1% is good, but Web SoS falling to 45.2% (–4.8pp vs target) suggests web acquisition — likely paid search and display — was being cut or was underperforming disproportionately during the crisis. If web paid was being consciously reduced to manage costs during the downturn, this is a managed retreat. If it was competitive pressure, it's a more serious signal.

- **New Projects MtD at +34.9% YoY during conflict conditions is remarkable resilience**: Even in the third week of a demand shock, the New Projects segment was running more than a third above the same period last year. The W13 recovery to +29% above weekly target confirms this segment exits the crisis faster than the rest of the business. The implication: developer relationships and off-plan product have a stickiness that secondary market activity lacks.
