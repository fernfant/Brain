# A Beginner's Guide to Using Claude (Helena Di Biase)

## Summary
A business-owner-oriented introduction to Claude, positioning it as a superior alternative to ChatGPT for practical work. Covers key features (Projects, Memory, Research Mode, Artifacts, Cowork, Connectors), the CRISP prompting framework, and a step-by-step implementation workflow. The guide stands out for its practical business use cases and honest caveats about Claude's limitations.

## Key Points
- Constitutional AI: trained on principles for thoughtful, nuanced responses — fewer hallucinations than competitors
- Key advantage over ChatGPT: prose quality (human-sounding, not "peppy/over-structured"), thinking depth, 1M token context window
- ChatGPT still wins on: image generation, established third-party integrations
- Projects: dedicated workspaces with documents, custom instructions, ongoing conversations
- Artifacts: interactive live content (calculators, flowcharts, quizzes) as shareable links — no coding required
- CRISP framework: Context, Role, Intent, Specifics, Perspective
- Pricing: Free / Pro £20/month / Max £100–200/month / Teams £25–30/seat
- Caveat: Claude lacks professional certifications; all output requires expert review for regulatory/legal/medical use

## Key Insights

- **The 1M token context window fundamentally changes what tasks Claude can handle**: 750,000 words of context means entire books, full codebases, or years of business documents can fit in a single session. This isn't just a spec improvement — it eliminates the chunk-and-retrieve architecture that RAG systems require for large documents. For knowledge-intensive work, the practical ceiling moved from "summaries of summaries" to "the whole document."

- **Artifacts as shareable no-code interactive tools is the most underappreciated Claude feature for business users**: A pricing calculator, intake form, or flowchart that lives at a shareable link — built in a conversation, no developer required — changes the economics of internal tooling. What previously required developer time now requires a good prompt. The output is live and interactive, not a static document.

- **The CRISP framework's "Perspective" element (reader context) is what most prompting guides omit**: Most frameworks cover what you want (Role, Specifics) and why (Intent). CRISP adds the reader's perspective — who will receive this output and what they care about. This is the element that makes business communications feel targeted rather than generic, and it's the hardest to teach because it requires theory-of-mind about the audience.
