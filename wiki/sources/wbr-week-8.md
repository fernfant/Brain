# WBR Week 8 (Feb)

## Summary
Late February: commercial forecast at 89% (excl. Hamptons deal moved to March), with March looking strong at 96–100%. Exclusive Listings rebaselining entered its live phase — clean data expected by March 6. The standout competitive event of the week was Bayut's Stake Series B announcement, which dominated PR and dropped PF's PR SoV from 99.1% to 78.9%. New Projects continued its strong run at +26.6% above target.

## Key Points
- Sales forecast: 89% (excl. Hamptons deal moved to March); March forecast 96–100%
- New Projects: $187.6K (+26.6% vs target); MtD +123% YoY
- Exclusive Listings rebaselining: 7 initiatives; live data expected Mar 6; includes WA agent reminders and Bayut auto-import by Mar 16
- SEO SoV: 41.8% (+0.2pp WoW)
- PR SoV: 78.9% — dropped from 99.1% due to Bayut Stake Series B media coverage
- Google Play rating: 4.5 → 4.2 (~300 five-star ratings removed as spam)
- Login via Mobile + WhatsApp launching Mar 4 (expected +5% login uplift in Q1)

## Key Insights

- **Bayut's Stake Series B is a strategic signal, not just a PR event**: A Series B from Stake (a Bayut-linked entity) means Bayut is actively raising capital and signalling growth ambitions. The fact that their funding round commanded enough media coverage to tank PF's PR SoV from 99.1% to 78.9% in a single week shows the competitive narrative is shifting. PF needs a proactive investor and partner story of its own, not just reactive PR.

- **The Exclusive Listings rebaselining timeline (live by Mar 6, Bayut auto-import by Mar 16) is tight against the conflict**: These initiatives were scheduled to deliver clean data and new supply just as the March shock hit. The conflict disruption in W10 meant the rebaselining's impact — both positive (cleaner data) and negative (lower reported count) — was mixed into the conflict noise, making it impossible to cleanly evaluate the initiative's success.

- **New Projects MtD at +123% YoY is the best sustained run in the data**: Three consecutive weeks above target ($171.4K, $187.6K, and a strong Feb monthly of $707.4K) confirm this isn't a one-off. The New Projects segment was performing at a level that made the March W10 crash (–42% WoW) especially jarring — the fall was from a high base, not a weak one.
