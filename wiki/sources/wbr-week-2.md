# WBR Week 2 (Jan 5–11)

## Summary
The first full WBR of the year. Dubai's real estate market opened positively at +4% YoY, but the growth was uneven — Primary led at +9% while Secondary declined –4%, a divergence that would persist through the quarter. Commercially, the business was tracking at 98% of a AED 63.5M target. The headline operational challenge was Exclusive Listings, stagnant at 11.7K for three consecutive weeks, while web paid traffic lagged as new campaigns scaled.

## Key Points
- DLD Sale Value: AED 15.2Bn (+4% YoY); Primary +9%, Secondary –4%
- Sales forecast: 98% of AED 63.5M target
- SEO SoV: 41.6% (+1.1pp WoW) — improving trajectory
- Net Exclusive Listings: 11.7K — stagnated for 3 consecutive weeks
- Web paid sessions: –5.4% vs target; recovery expected by week 3
- App leads: +12.7% YoY; CR at 25.2% (+4.5pp vs target)
- Actions in progress: NB sales initiatives, exclusive listings targets, CRM data cleanup

## Key Insights

- **Primary–Secondary divergence from day one**: Primary grew +9% YoY while Secondary fell –4% in the very first week of the year, suggesting off-plan demand was already meaningfully stronger than resale. This divergence held throughout Q1 and became even more pronounced after the March conflict shock, making Primary the structural growth engine of the Dubai market.

- **App vs web paid is a persistent gap, not a one-week blip**: With app leads up +12.7% YoY and CR at 25.2% while web paid sessions lagged –5.4% vs target, the channel gap appeared in week 2 and never fully closed across the quarter. This isn't a campaign ramp-up issue — it's a structural shift in where engaged users live.

- **Exclusive Listings stagnation is an early warning signal**: Three consecutive weeks flat at 11.7K, well below the eventual 23K January target, indicates the supply capture problem predated any competitor action. The stagnation in week 2 foreshadowed the entire quarter's recurring listings gap story.
