# WBR Week 9 — February Monthly Lookback

## Summary
February closed as a strong month despite a commercial result of 88.4% (excl. Hamptons). DLD market growth moderated to +19% YoY, but New Projects delivered $707.4K (+22% above target) and Media & Developers overachieved by 56% — with an Aldar contract of AED 1.8M. App installs nearly doubled their target at 107K vs 60K. The March forecast of ~72% already reflected geopolitical headwinds, signalling awareness of external risk before the conflict fully hit in W10.

## Key Points
- DLD Sale: +19% YoY (Primary +38%, Secondary –1%)
- Commercial: AED 55.3M (88.4% vs target, excl. Hamptons moved to March)
- SEO SoS: 47.4% — below target but +1.6pp MoM improvement
- New Projects: $707.4K (+22% above target); 7.7K sold leads
- Media & Developers: +56% above target; Aldar contract AED 1.8M
- App installs: 107K (vs 60K target); MAU: 454K (vs 432K target)
- Android rating: now 4.07 (recovering from 4.03 low)
- Mobile INP: 70.2% (+1.1pp) — still below 72% target
- March forecast: ~72% (geopolitical headwinds flagged)

## Key Insights

- **The March forecast of ~72% at the end of February shows conflict awareness, not conflict surprise**: Someone in the business already knew heading into March that geopolitical conditions would suppress results. This contextualises the W10 shock differently — it wasn't an out-of-nowhere event; it was a risk that was being monitored and partially priced into the forecast. The question is whether 72% was conservative enough (actual March closed at 66%) or whether the conflict escalated faster than modelled.

- **App installs at 107K vs a 60K target represents a systematic forecast error**: Beating the install target by 78% in a single month suggests either the target was set too conservatively or consumer acquisition was dramatically outperforming expectations. Either way, a 78% beat on MAU targets (454K vs 432K) in the same month reinforces this — and implies that Q2 app metrics targets were probably set against this inflated base, creating a harder comp.

- **Media & Developers +56% above target with an Aldar AED 1.8M contract is the quarter's hidden outperformer**: This segment barely appears in the WBR narrative but it overachieved massively in February. When new developer budgets were put on hold in W10 due to the conflict, the month-to-month reversal would have been sharp. The Aldar contract is also notable — it represents the kind of anchor deal that can compress or expand depending on developer sentiment during geopolitical uncertainty.
