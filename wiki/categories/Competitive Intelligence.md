## Key Insights

- **Bayut is playing offence while PF manages a crisis**: In the same weeks that PF was dealing with the conflict shock (W10–W13), Bayut raised a Series B (W8), held its iOS app store ranking at #4 while PF fell to #7 (W13), and remained active in signing new-to-market exclusive listings (W4). A competitor that is fundraising, maintaining app store presence, and acquiring supply during your crisis will emerge with structural advantages when conditions normalise.

- **The 61% organic Sale traffic share is PF's strongest competitive moat — and the only one clearly quantified in the data**: Across all the WBR data, Exclusive Listings is short of target, eLPL is below benchmark, NPS is at 3, and app store rankings are volatile. The one area where PF's competitive position is unambiguously strong is SEO Sale keyword dominance. This makes defending and extending this position more strategically important, not less, especially given the 6pp gain (from ~55% to 61%) achieved in the past year.

- **The 29% listing gap to competition (cited in W3) was never closed**: Exclusive Listings ended January at 16K vs a 23K target, and the rebaselining announced in February was a data quality correction, not a supply capture improvement. If the 29% gap existed in January, and Bayut was actively adding supply in the same period (W4), the gap likely widened through Q1. Supply parity with Bayut is a precondition for eLPL parity — and eLPL is what clients see in their dashboards.

- **Bayut's Stake Series B announcement caused PF's PR SoV to fall 20pp in one week**: The competitive narrative in the market — which developers, investors, and agents read — shifted materially in W8 when Bayut's funding round dominated coverage. PR SoV is a weak metric for marketing effectiveness, but a strong indicator of who is setting the competitive agenda. Bayut controlled the narrative in February; PF was reactive.

- **App store rankings are an underappreciated competitive battleground**: In W13, PF dropped from #4 to #7 in iOS Lifestyle while Bayut held at #4. For users browsing the App Store for a UAE real estate app, ranking #4 vs #7 means meaningfully different discovery rates. App store position is partially within PF's control (ratings strategy, ASO) and partially policy-driven — but the Q2 plan to address it needs to treat it as a front in the competitive battle, not just a product health metric.
