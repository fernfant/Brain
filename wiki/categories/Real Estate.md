## Key Insights

- **The UAE real estate market in Q1 2026 ran two distinct stories**: January–February saw extraordinary headline growth (+58% YoY DLD in January, +19% in February), driven primarily by Primary (off-plan) and partially by luxury and land transactions. Stripping those out, normalised growth was +30%. March brought a sharp reversal to –24% YoY. These two periods within a single quarter make rolling averages meaningless — the market needs to be understood as pre-conflict and post-conflict.

- **Primary and Secondary have decoupled as performance stories**: Primary led growth throughout January–February (Primary +65% YoY in January vs Secondary +52%) and absorbed the conflict shock better (Primary Plus campaigns –3% vs New Projects –42% in W10). Secondary market is more sentiment-sensitive, tied to individual buyer confidence, and dropped both in January vs its growth rate and again post-conflict. Treating the Dubai market as a single entity obscures this structural split.

- **PF's SEO dominance in Sale keywords is strategically more valuable than it appears**: 61% organic traffic share in the Sale segment, which generates 85% of all commissions, means roughly half of all commission-generating organic search traffic in UAE real estate flows through PF. This creates a competitive moat that is hard to displace — but only if PF continues investing to defend and extend it, which requires ongoing SEO spend even when commercial conditions are difficult.

- **Exclusive Listings as a metric was never on solid footing this quarter**: Starting at 11.7K in January (vs a 23K target), jumping to 16K on approvals, then requiring a formal rebaselining to remove non-compliant and duplicate entries — the metric's integrity was questionable for much of Q1. Any competitive benchmarking against Bayut's supply count is complicated by the fact that PF's own figure was under correction throughout the period.

- **The commission pool concentration in Sale (85% vs 15% Rent) means market shocks hit PF asymmetrically**: A real estate market shock driven by conflict — which primarily hits buyer confidence and transaction decisions — lands hardest on Sale. Rent is more resilient (people need to live somewhere) and generates only 15% of commissions. PF's heavy exposure to the highest-value but most volatile segment means its revenue is more cyclical than a more Rent-balanced competitor would be.
