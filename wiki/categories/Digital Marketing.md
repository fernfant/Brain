## Key Insights

- **SEO is the most resilient marketing channel in a crisis**: Across 13 weeks, SEO SoV improved from 41.6% in W2 to 45.8% by W11–W12, including a +3.9pp gain in W10 — the single most disruptive week of the quarter. Paid media pulled back during the conflict; organic held and strengthened. This counter-cyclical property makes SEO investment particularly valuable as a base layer of acquisition that functions even when discretionary marketing spend contracts.

- **The measurement gap between SEO SoV (~45.8%) and the dedicated SEO report's organic traffic share (~61%) signals a methodology problem**: The WBR SEO SoV metric and the March 2026 SEO snapshot report are measuring something different, or using different data sources (SEMrush vs SimilarWeb vs internal). A 15pp gap in reported SEO performance between internal and external measurement should be resolved — otherwise it's impossible to know which number to trust when making investment decisions.

- **App SoS consistently outperforming Web SoS represents a structural channel shift**: From the January monthly (App SoS 56.4% vs Web SoS 49.6%) through March (App SoS 57–58% vs Web SoS 45–46%), the app channel increasingly dominates organic sessions. This is consistent with global mobile-first trends, but it also concentrates traffic on a single surface — and that surface is subject to app store ranking volatility and platform policy risk (demonstrated three times in one quarter).

- **Web SoS declining to 45.2% by W12 despite strong SEO SoV is a channel mix story**: Strong organic keyword rankings don't automatically translate to strong overall web sessions if paid web is being pulled back simultaneously. The widening gap between App SoS and Web SoS likely reflects a combination of deliberate investment shift toward app and reduced web paid during the conflict period. This matters because web-based users and app users have different profiles and conversion funnels.

- **PR Share of Voice is a volatile, event-driven metric that can swing 20pp in a week**: PF's PR SoV was at 99.1% one week (W7) and 78.9% the next (W8) — entirely because of Bayut's Stake Series B announcement. A metric with that kind of external sensitivity cannot be used for marketing effectiveness measurement. PR SoV is better understood as a competitive narrative indicator: who is generating media gravity this week.
