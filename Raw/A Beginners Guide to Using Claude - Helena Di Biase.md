# A Beginner's Guide to Using Claude

## Overview

Helena Di Biase's comprehensive guide explains Claude, an AI assistant built by Anthropic, positioning it as a superior alternative to ChatGPT for business owners seeking practical AI implementation.

## What is Claude?

Claude employs "Constitutional AI"—trained on principles guiding its behavior toward thoughtful, nuanced responses. The platform avoids hallucinations more effectively than competitors and maintains human-like reasoning patterns.

## Key Differences from ChatGPT

- **Writing Quality**: Claude produces prose that sounds authentically human, avoiding the "peppy, over-structured" patterns characteristic of ChatGPT
- **Thinking Depth**: Claude considers trade-offs and asks clarifying questions that advance conversations meaningfully
- **Context Window**: Up to one million tokens (approximately 750,000 words)
- **Where ChatGPT Wins**: Image generation and established third-party integrations

## Essential Claude Features

**Projects**: Dedicated workspaces combining documents, custom instructions, and ongoing conversations.

**Memory**: Cross-conversation learning about user preferences and business context.

**Research Mode**: Deep web analysis synthesizing multiple sources into thorough reports.

**Artifacts**: Interactive, live content—pricing calculators, flowcharts, quizzes—published via shareable links without coding.

**Cowork**: Desktop application enabling autonomous multi-step task completion with folder access.

**Connectors**: Integration with Google Workspace, Slack, and other tools via Model Context Protocol.

## Custom Instructions Setup

Global instructions should specify business type, audience, tone preferences, language, and communication style.

Example framework: "I run [business type]. My audience is [description]. I write in [language]. My tone is [characteristics]. When [situation], [desired behavior]."

## Pricing Structure

- **Free Plan**: Limited daily messages, Sonnet model, memory, Artifacts
- **Pro (£20/month)**: 5x usage, all models including Opus, Projects, Research mode, Cowork
- **Max (£100-200/month)**: 5–20x Pro usage
- **Teams (£25-30/seat)**: Shared Projects, team integrations

## Effective Prompting: CRISP Method

**Context**: Establish business background and situation details.
**Role**: Specify desired expertise perspective.
**Intent**: Clarify actual goals beyond surface requests.
**Specifics**: Define length, tone, format, inclusions, exclusions.
**Perspective**: Identify reader context and concerns.

**Weak prompt**: "Help me with client onboarding."

**Strong prompt**: "I run nutrition coaching at £250/month minimum 12 weeks. Current process lacks structure; clients often skip questionnaires. Build a sequence covering payment confirmation through week two, with warm professional tone emphasizing client confidence, not clinical language. Flag gaps."

## High-Impact Business Use Cases

- **Client Experience**: Onboarding sequences, welcome packages, communication templates
- **Research & Competitor Intelligence**: Pricing analysis, review mining, trend research
- **Financial Modeling**: Revenue data analysis, pricing scenarios, cash flow projections
- **Custom Tools**: Interactive calculators, intake questionnaires as Artifacts

## Implementation Workflow

1. Sign up for free plan
2. Set global custom instructions (5-minute task)
3. Review memory settings
4. Create first project for your biggest time sink
5. Upload relevant documents
6. Start with one task (typically client onboarding)
7. Expand to additional projects

## Important Caveats

Claude lacks professional certifications and cannot guarantee accuracy on regulatory, legal, or medical matters. All output requires expert review before client-facing use.
