## Key Takeaways

1. **The quarter had two fundamentally different regimes.** January–February metrics should not be used as a baseline for Q2 planning without adjusting for the conflict shock. Conversely, the March trough likely overstates structural weakness.

2. **SEO is the resilient channel.** It improved consistently throughout Jan–Feb and held through the conflict. Investment here has compounding returns and low volatility.

3. **Exclusive Listings supply gap is structural.** The 7K gap to target in January, combined with Bayut actively signing new-to-market inventory, indicates a competitive disadvantage that relief packages won't fix.

4. **New Projects is a strong business.** It outperformed consistently in Jan–Feb, absorbed the biggest shock in March, and is already recovering fastest. It deserves continued investment and attention.

5. **NPS declined significantly before the conflict.** A B2C NPS of 3 in January, down 10 YoY, is a product health signal that could be masked by usage growth metrics.

6. **App store ranking instability is a Q2 risk.** Three separate platform policy incidents (Feb Google spam removal, Feb iOS Luciq, April Google Play policy change) suggest the app ranking strategy needs to be more proactive and less reactive.

7. **Data infrastructure is not keeping pace with the business.** Kochava, CHEQ, SEMrush, and Redshift discrepancies appeared in at least 5 of 13 weeks. Reliable metrics are a prerequisite for good decision-making at this pace.
