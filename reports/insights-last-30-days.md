# Key Insights — Last 30 Days (Mar–Apr 2026)

## Context

The defining event of this period was the **US-Iran conflict**, which erupted in early March. Week 10 captured the initial shock; weeks 11–13 track the recovery arc. The narrative is one of impact containment and gradual stabilisation, not full recovery.

## Revenue & Commercial

- **April forecast: 53%** (upside 55%). Well below historical run-rates. Ongoing hostilities are directly driving churn and suppressing upside.
- **March closed at 66%** of target. A significant miss driven almost entirely by the geopolitical shock, not structural business issues.
- **March Relief + Eid Relief packages** are active; 79% of the client book is in active conversations about relief. This is both a retention mechanism and a revenue deferral risk.
- **New Projects is recovering strongly**: from –42% revenue WoW in W10, to 102% of target in W11, to **+29% above target** ($168.9K) in W13. This is the clearest green shoot in the data.

## Key Takeaways

1. **New Projects is leading the recovery** — it took the biggest hit and is now the biggest positive signal.
2. **Web SoS needs attention**: it's consistently trailing App SoS and sitting below target. App-first positioning may be masking a web deterioration story.
3. **Relief packages are the sales strategy right now** — 79% client coverage means nearly the entire book is on some form of crisis pricing. The revenue impact when these roll off needs to be modelled.
4. **App store rankings are fragile**: both platforms saw major rank volatility due to policy changes, not competitor action. Q2 rating strategy is a priority.
5. **eLPL baselines need resetting** — the Q2 targets will not reflect pre-conflict levels. Any performance review in Q2 must account for the new baselines.
